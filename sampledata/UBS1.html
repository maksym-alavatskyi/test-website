SABMiller

Q2FY lager volumes under pressure

SAB will report Q2FY trading update on 14 October

SAB is lapping its most challenging comparable of 2015FY in Q2FY with +3% organic lager volume reported in Q214FY. Performance the prior year was inflated by South Africa (lager volumes +6% of which 2% points due to trade-load ahead of strikes) and China (lager volumes +10%). We forecast Q2 15FYE organic lager volume decline of -1% (0% for H1 15FYE) and +3% organic Net Producer Revenue (NPR) growth y/y, with NPR/hl continuing to run at +3% y/y in Q215FYE. We assume H1 15FYE organic NPR growth is +4.5% y/y.

We expect Q2FY negative lager volumes in all regions except Latam and Africa

We expect Q2FYE Europe lager volumes to decline by -4% as a weak macro, unfavourable weather and high competition continue to weigh. We expect MillerCoors STRs -1.5% y/y. Asia lager will be weak with lager -1% (flat China volumes against a tough comp and poor weather, and -1% in Australia). SA lager volumes will decline -1% against a very challenging comparable. However, in Latam we forecast +2% lager volume growth y/y with an easy Colombia comparable due to national strikes and social unrest the prior year, as well as good NPR/hl growth. We forecast continued subdued lager volume growth in Africa (+3%) with Zambia declining, Tanzania and Castel still struggling, Zimbabwe lapping a very easy comparable but very tough macro, while Nigeria should still see good growth and Mozambique is stabilising.

Currency headwind from Rand and Colombian Peso to US$ earnings

We have updated our US$ EPS for the recent Rand (11.3 from 10.6 now assumed on average for 15FYE) and COP weakness (2,026 from 1,922), hence lower US$ EPS by -4%. However, we note that the US$ strength relative to Sterling partly mitigates this. For 2015FY UBSe +5.5% group organic sales and +7.7% organic EBIT growth y/y.

Valuation: Neutral, 3500p price target (DCF-based)

SAB trades on 20.1 2015CYE P/E, a 15% sector premium. SAB hosts one of its divisional seminars today (6 October) on Marketing).

Statement of Risk

Sector-specific risks: (1) consumer expenditure levels and the economic and political stability in the countries of operation; (2) governmental and regulatory risk of excise duty increases, or restrictions such as advertising or deposit laws over which the industry can exercise limited influence; (3) weather in any given year due to the seasonality of consumption above all for beer and soft drinks; (4) shifts in consumer trends between categories and substitution risk from other competing products, namely wine and spirits; (5) competition and the impact on returns from increased A&P and capital investment; (6) variability of input costs such as raw materials (e.g. barley, malt, sugar and agave) and packaging materials (e.g. glass, aluminium, and PET) as well as fuel costs on distribution; and (7) increasing cost of consolidation as asset values continue to increase, thereby adversely affecting the level of industry returns.
Company-specific risks are: (1) c76% of profits are generated in Emerging markets. Emerging markets offer better growth, but do not go up in a straight line. (2) with some 19% of profit generated in South Africa, cash flows and valuation are in part driven by the US$/rand exchange rate; (3) Altria (Philip Morris) owns 27% of the economic interest in SABMiller, the lock-up on which ended on 30 June 2005; (4) some of the group’s growth depends on improvements in price/mix which leaves SABMiller more exposed to macro and competitive threats, particularly in South Africa, Australia and Eastern Europe. (5) SABMiller has a strong balance sheet and the use of this capacity will be central to the future value of the company.
